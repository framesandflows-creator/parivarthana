Introduction

A zero-waste campus system with people at the center

Parivarthana was established in 2008 within CHRIST (Deemed to be University) to transform waste into useful resources and promote responsible waste management across campus. Its core objective is to sustain a zero-waste ecosystem through recycling, repurposing, treatment, and circular use.

The initiative is driven by dedicated women staff, supported by student volunteers and university administration. Alongside operational recycling, Parivarthana advances the 3Rs, eco-clubs, campus awareness, and sustainability education for students and faculty.

Core Units

Each system converts a different waste stream into value

Biogas

Wet waste becomes fuel

  • Wet waste is processed into biogas using two 150 kg tanks and one 200 kg tank.
  • Daily LPG production reaches about 25 kg.
  • Annual LPG output exceeds 5,500-6,000 kg, valued at over ₹5 lakhs.

Water Treatment Plant

Used water is purified and reused

  • Water is treated to an approximate purity level of 98%.
  • Recovered water supports vegetation and gardening across campus spaces.
  • Average recycling volume reaches roughly 1.5 to 2 lakh liters daily.

Composting

Organic matter turns into soil value

  • Wet waste and plant debris decompose into organic compost.
  • Four composting tanks, each with 1,500 kg capacity, support the process.
  • About 2,500-3,000 kg of compost is generated annually, with nearly ₹1 lakh in value.

Paper Recycling Unit

Campus paper waste becomes handmade products

  • Answer sheets, question papers, newspapers, and other paper waste are recovered.
  • The unit converts collected paper into handmade utility and stationery products.
  • Production capacity reaches nearly 2 lakh recycled products every month.

Waste Segregation

The sorting engine behind the entire model

  • Waste is collected from bins placed at multiple campus points.
  • The team separates wet and dry waste for bio-recycling and recovery streams.
  • More than 70,000 kg of dry waste annually contributes around ₹5,00,000 in revenue.

SHG At Parivarthana

Environmental work with empowerment built in

Parivarthana’s team culture highlights unity through a diverse group of dedicated women. In 2019, a Self-Help Group was established within the Women Common Interest Group to strengthen financial stability, shared learning, and holistic empowerment.

This SHG deepens the project beyond waste management. It turns the campus model into a collaborative livelihood ecosystem where sustainability, dignity, and professional growth move together.
